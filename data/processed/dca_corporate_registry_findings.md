This is consistent with a very recently formed entity (publicly launched February 2025) that may not yet have filed its first Form 990 or whose state filing is not yet indexed in public databases.

## 6. Funding and Transparency Issues

### Meta Funding

Multiple news sources (Bloomberg/Insurance Journal, July 2025; Deseret News, December 2025; The Center Square/Louisiana, May 2025) report that Meta is helping fund the Digital Childhood Alliance:

- **Bloomberg (July 2025):** "Meta is also helping to fund the Digital Childhood Alliance, a coalition of conservative groups leading efforts to pass app-store age verification, according to three people familiar with the funding."
- **Louisiana Senate Finance Committee hearing:** When Senator Jay Morris pressed Executive Director Casey Stefanski on whether tech companies fund DCA, she "eventually confirmed that they do but refused to name them."
- Stefanski also stated that "the father of the Digital Childhood Alliance's founder" is its largest donor.
- DCA's 501(c)(4) status means it is not required to publicly disclose its donors.

### Characterization

The organization has been described as:
- A "coalition of conservative groups" (Bloomberg/Insurance Journal)
- An "astroturf operation" where "corporate money creates the illusion of grassroots activism" (Deseret News, citing investigative reporting)
- Backed by "over 140 organizations"

## 7. Gaps and Limitations

1. **No state incorporation record found.** The specific state of incorporation for Digital Childhood Alliance, Inc. could not be determined. DC DCRA, Colorado SOS, Virginia SCC, and Delaware corporate databases were searched or attempted without success.

2. **No EIN found.** The organization's Employer Identification Number is not publicly available through any database searched.

3. **No Form 990 filed.** As a 501(c)(4) formed circa late 2024/early 2025, DCA's first Form 990 would likely not be due until late 2025 or 2026, and would not yet appear in ProPublica or other databases.

4. **No officers/directors list found.** Beyond Casey Stefanski (Executive Director) and Melissa McKay (DCI founder), no board members or officers are publicly identified for either entity.

5. **No registered agent identified.** Could not access state corporate filing portals to determine the registered agent.

6. **Adam Eichberg connection unconfirmed.** While Eichberg has deep ties to the Arabella network (New Venture Fund, Windward Fund), no public source connects him to DCA. This does not rule out an undisclosed connection.

7. **Arabella address connection unconfirmed.** No evidence that DCA operates from or shares an address with any Arabella entity at 1828 L St NW, Washington, DC.

8. **Several web sources blocked.** WebFetch was denied access to multiple key sources including The Center Square, The Hayride, Jessica Reed Kraus Substack, CyberScoop, PR Newswire, IRS TEOS, and DC DCRA CorpOnline, limiting the depth of findings.

## 8. Summary of Key Findings

- **DCA is formally named "Digital Childhood Alliance, Inc."** and claims 501(c)(4) status, but no incorporation record, EIN, or Form 990 could be located in any public database. **UPDATE:** Subsequent IRS BMF search of all 4 regional extracts (eo1-eo4.csv) confirmed DCA is NOT in the IRS system. See `dca_incorporation_updated.md`.
- **DCA was publicly launched on February 25, 2025** and is headquartered in Washington, DC (remote organization, no public street address).
- **Casey Stefanski is Executive Director;** previously at NCOSE for 10 years. The "founder" appears to be a separate, unnamed individual whose father is DCA's largest donor.
- **Meta is funding DCA** according to multiple news reports, which Stefanski confirmed under questioning but refused to name specific donors.
- **No connection to Adam Eichberg or Headwaters Strategies** was publicly documented, though Eichberg's role as chair of the Arabella-managed New Venture Fund is notable given the opacity of DCA's organizational structure.
- **Headwaters Strategies, Inc.** is confirmed registered in Colorado (founded 2009, 1660 Lincoln St., Denver) but no link to DCA was found.
- **The Digital Childhood Institute (DCI)** is the 501(c)(3) sister entity, founded by Melissa McKay, who serves as President and Board Chair. **UPDATE:** DCI confirmed in IRS BMF with EIN 39-3684798, Delaware incorporation.

## 9. Subsequent Findings (Added 2026-03-13)

The following findings were established after this initial registry search:

- **NCOSE has a 501(c)(4) affiliate** — "NCOSEAction" / Institute for Public Policy (EIN 88-1180705), IRS ruling May 2025, same address and leadership as NCOSE. Potential DCA fiscal sponsor.
- **DCA processes donations through For Good DAF** (EIN 68-0480736) — classified as "Project" (ID 258136) in the system, not as a standalone nonprofit. For Good explicitly limits grants to 501(c)(3) organizations.
- **For Good DAF grants searched:** 59,736 recipients across 5 years ($1.73B) — zero matches for DCA, DCI, or NCOSE entities.
- **DCA's legal non-existence** (no EIN, no incorporation, no IRS record) is consistent with fiscal sponsorship under NCOSEAction or Sixteen Thirty Fund.
